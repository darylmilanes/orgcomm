Why It Matters

Organizational communication is the process by which people within an organization exchange information to achieve common goals.

  • Promotes teamwork and productivity
  • Reduces misunderstandings and conflicts
  • Enhances workplace culture and trust

Ambiguous workplace phrases (examples from the PDF)

“You need to improve your presentation.”

Problem: vague — no specifics or deadline.

Clear rewrite: “Please shorten the slides to 10, highlight the key findings, and practice eye contact. Present a revised version by Thursday 3 PM.”

“Let’s make sure someone takes care of the materials.”

Problem: unclear role assignment.

Clear rewrite: “Paula, can you print and prepare the workshop materials by Thursday morning?”

“I’ll get back to you as soon as possible.”

Problem: non-specific time phrasing.

Clear rewrite: “I’ll review this and update you by 2 PM tomorrow.”

“Please review the attached file and let me know your thoughts.”

Problem: broad request without scope or deadline.

Clear rewrite: “Please check if the tone and format match our branding and send comments by Friday.”

“Let’s have a meeting to talk about the project.”

Problem: unclear objective.

Clear rewrite: “Let’s meet at 10 AM to finalize the timeline and assign deliverables for Phase 2.”

“I’ll check it later.”

Problem: no timeframe; risks missed deadlines.

Clear rewrite: “I’ll check after lunch and send comments by 3 PM.”

“It’s just like last time.”

Problem: assumptive or incomplete message.

Clear rewrite: “It’s similar to the Q2 campaign focused on customer feedback, but this time our target is new clients. I’ll share the differences in the doc.”
